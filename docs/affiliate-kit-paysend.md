## 7. Content / Promotion strategy

```
Paysend is featured in three primary surfaces of BrasilConnect:

1. **Comparator page (/app#remessas)** — side-by-side comparison. Paysend is highlighted automatically when the user enters an amount above $1,000 because the flat $2 fee becomes the lowest-cost option. Click-through via /go/paysend.

2. **Editorial content (/guias/conta-bancaria, future /guias/transferencia-grande)** — recommended specifically for high-value transfers (property, business, emergencies).

3. **Push notifications** — opt-in notifications when Paysend runs first-transfer-free promo.

We do NOT use coupon-stuffing, toolbar pop-ups, PPC bidding on brand terms, or black-hat tactics.
```

## 8. Why Paysend specifically

```
Paysend is the natural fit for our audience for three reasons:

1. **Flat fee wins on large transfers — and we have those users** — most comparator sites focus on small remittances under $500. Our audience includes a meaningful tail of $1K-$10K transfers for property purchases in Brazil, business capital, and family emergencies. Paysend's $2 flat fee is mathematically the cheapest option in this band, and we surface the calculation transparently. Wise/Remitly users overpay here.

2. **Card-to-PIX is unique product positioning** — Paysend's USD-card → PIX-in-Brazil flow is faster than ACH-based competitors and doesn't require linking a US bank account. This matters for early-stage immigrants who haven't opened a US checking account yet but already have a debit card.

3. **Brand is European-trusted, low-key marketing** — many of our users have heard of Paysend through European backpacker friends or Estonian/UK contacts. The brand has credibility without the heavy advertising overhead that pushes WU/MoneyGram fees higher.

We want to grow with Paysend's expanding US presence in the Brazil corridor.
```
